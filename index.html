How Dubs Become NBA's Greatest Dynasty?

From a Rebuild to Winning Four Championships in Eight Years

How Warriors went from ground down days to NBA dynasty:

  • 2009 - Warriors Draft Guard Stephen Curry with the #7 Overall pick in 2009 NBA Draft
  • 2011 - Two years later, Warriors draft Guard Klay Thompson, who would later become Curry's "Splash Brother"
  • 2012 - Warriors Draft Forward Draymond Green, the "Heart and Soul" of the Warriors
  • May 2013 - After missing the playoffs for 5 consecutive seasons, the Warriors young core makes the playoffs and defeats the Denver Nuggets for only their second playoff series win in 22 years.
  • July 2013 - Forward Andre Iguodala officialy joined the Warrriors in a three-team sign-and-trade deal.
  • April 2014 - For the first time since 1994, the Dubs franchise wins 50 regular season games.
  • July 2014 - Warriors sign Head Coach Steve Kerr
  • 2015 - Lead by 2015 MVP Stephen Curry and his "Splash Brother" Klay Thompson, the Warriors become the NBA Champions for the first time since 1975 defeating Lebron James and the Cleveland Cavaliers.
  • April 2016 - After winning the title in 2015 the Warriors come back the next year and have the Greatest regular season in NBA history going 73-9 breaking the previous record of 72 wins by the '96 Chicago Bulls. Stephen Curry gets his second MVP and the first ever unanimous MVP decision in NBA History.
  • June 2016 - Warriors throw a 3-1 Lead in the NBA Finals to the Cleveland Cavaliers.
  • July 2016 - Former MVP Kevin Durant signs With the Golden State Warriors
  • 2017 - Warriors goes for 16-1 in the Playoffs, NBA record and reclaim the NBA Title. Kevin Durant wins his first NBA Championship and Finals MVP.
  • 2018 - The Dubs are Back-to-Back Champions.
  • 2019 - The Warriors went to the Finals for Four (4) consecutive years. Kevin Durant and Klay Thompson went down with injuries in Game 6 of the NBA Finals. Warriors lose to the Raptors 4-2.
  • July 2019 - Kevin Durant leaves the Warriors to Brooklyn via sign-and-trade. Warriors receive All-star D'Angelo Russell in return. The Dubs also traded 2015 Finals MVP Andre Iguoudala to the Grizzlies. Warriors selected Guard Jordan Poole with the #28 Pick of 2019 NBA Draft.
  • October 2019 - Steph Curry broke his hand which sidelined him for the remainder of the season
  • February 2020 - Warriors traded Russel to Timberwolves for former #1 pick Forward Andrew Wiggins and future 1st round pick which later become #7 Pick in 2021 NBA Draft.
  • February 2020 - Is this the END of DYNASTY? The NBA was stopped due to outbreak of Corona-Virus. The Warriors went with the worst record in the NBA during that year winning only 15 games in the regular season due to Curry, Thompson and Green's injuries.
  • July 2020 - Klay Thompson suffers a torn Achilles tendon during his ACL rehab which sidelined him for another season. With the #2 pick in the NBA Draft, he Warriors select Center James Wiseman.
  • February 2021 - The rise of Jordan Poole. After playing for the Santa Cruz Warriors, G-League affiliate of Dubs, Poole had a breakout season.
  • May 2021 - The #8th seeded Warriors lose to the #9th seeded Memphis Grizzlies in the OT of the Play-in Game for 2021 NBA playoffs.
  • July 2021 - The Warriors select F Jonathan Kuminga and G/F Moses Moody with the #7, #14 pick, respectively in the 2021 NBA Draft. Forward Andre Iguodala returns to the Warriors on a minimum deal.
  • December 2021 - 2 Time MVP Stephen Curry break the NBA Record for three pointers made in his career passing Ray Allen with 2974 threes.
  • January 2022 - After missing 941 days, Klay Thompson reunites with his Splash Brother and the Dubs for the quest of another title run.
  • May 2022 - The RETURN of DYNASTY? Lead by first-ever Western Conference Finals MVP Stephen Curry. The Warriors have defeated the Boston Celtics in NBA Finals, 4-2 winning the Four (4) Championships in the last Eight (8) Years.Also, Future Hall of Famer and Two-time MVP Stephen Curry has won his first NBA Finals MVP.
